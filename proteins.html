Proteins: The Building Blocks of Life

What Are Proteins?

Proteins are large, complex molecules that play many critical roles in the body. They are made up of hundreds or thousands of smaller units called amino acids, which are attached to one another in long chains.

Functions of Proteins

  • Growth and Maintenance: Essential for building and repairing tissues
  • Enzymes: Facilitate biochemical reactions
  • Hormones: Act as chemical messengers
  • Immune Function: Form antibodies to fight infection
  • Transport and Storage: Carry molecules throughout the body

Best Protein Sources

Animal Sources

  • Chicken breast
  • Eggs
  • Fish (salmon, tuna)
  • Greek yogurt
  • Lean beef

Plant Sources

  • Lentils
  • Chickpeas
  • Quinoa
  • Almonds
  • Tofu

Daily Protein Requirements

The Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) for protein is 0.8 grams per kilogram of body weight. Athletes and active individuals may need 1.2-2.0 grams per kilogram.
