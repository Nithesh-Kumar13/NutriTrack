Fats: Essential for Health

Understanding Dietary Fats

Fats are an essential part of our diet and important for good health. They provide energy, help absorb vitamins, and are important for proper growth and development.

Types of Fats

Healthy Fats

  • Monounsaturated: Olive oil, avocados, nuts
  • Polyunsaturated: Fatty fish, walnuts, flaxseeds
  • Omega-3s: Salmon, chia seeds, walnuts

Unhealthy Fats

  • Trans fats: Fried foods, baked goods
  • Saturated fats: Red meat, butter (limit intake)

Functions of Fats

  • Energy storage
  • Cell membrane structure
  • Hormone production
  • Brain function and development
  • Absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)

Recommended Daily Intake

20-35% of total daily calories should come from fats, with less than 10% from saturated fats.
