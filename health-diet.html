Know about Nutrients

Carbohydrates

The primary role of carbohydrates is to supply energy to all cells in the body. Many cells prefer glucose as a source of energy versus other compounds like fatty acids. Some cells, such as red blood cells, are only able to produce cellular energy from glucose.

Fruits like Apples, bananas, berries, mangoes, melons, oranges. Starchy vegetables like white and sweet potatoes, peas, and corn. Dairy products like milk and yogurt are rich at carbohydrates.

Proteins

Proteins serve as structural support, biochemical catalysts, hormones, enzymes, building blocks, and initiators of cellular death.

Seeds like Almonds, pine nuts, walnuts, macadamias, hazelnuts, cashews, pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds, and sunflower seeds. Sea food like Fish, prawns, crab, lobster, mussels, oysters, scallops, clams, trout, perch, shrimp. And meat items like chicken, pork, lean beaf, eggs are rich in Proteins.

Vitamins

Vitamins are essential nutrients that help the body grow, develop, and function normally. They help with a variety of functions like:

  • Immune system: Vitamins help the body fight infection and disease.
  • Bones and teeth: Vitamins help strengthen bones and teeth, and aid in calcium absorption.
  • Skin: Vitamins help maintain healthy skin.
  • Vision: Vitamins help maintain healthy vision.
  • Metabolism: Vitamins help the body metabolize proteins, carbs, nucleic acids, and fats.

Egg, Brocolli, Avacodo, Carrot, Citrus fruits, spinach are best examples of Vitamin Food.

Minerals

Minerals are important for many bodily functions, including building bones and teeth, making enzymes and hormones, and supporting brain function.

nuts, seeds, eggs, shellfish, leafy greens, and cocoa are minerals rich foods.

Fats

Fats play many important roles in the body, including providing energy, protecting organs, and supporting cell growth.

Avacodos, Full Fat yogurt, Chia seeds, Fatty Fish, Dark Chocolate and olive oil are healthy fat-rich foods.
