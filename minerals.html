Minerals: Essential for Body Functions

What Are Minerals?

Minerals are inorganic elements that come from the earth and are absorbed by plants or eaten by animals. Your body needs minerals to perform various functions, from building strong bones to transmitting nerve impulses.

Types of Minerals

Macrominerals (Needed in larger amounts)

  • Calcium: Bones, muscle function
  • Magnesium: Energy production, nerve function
  • Potassium: Fluid balance, heart function
  • Sodium: Fluid balance, nerve impulses

Trace Minerals (Needed in small amounts)

  • Iron: Oxygen transport
  • Zinc: Immune function, wound healing
  • Iodine: Thyroid function
  • Selenium: Antioxidant

Key Minerals and Their Sources

Calcium

Dairy products, leafy greens, fortified foods

Iron

Red meat, beans, fortified cereals

Magnesium

Nuts, seeds, whole grains

Zinc

Meat, shellfish, legumes

Signs of Mineral Deficiency

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Muscle cramps
  • Poor concentration
  • Brittle nails and hair
  • Irregular heartbeat
